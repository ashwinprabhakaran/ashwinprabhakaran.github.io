About me

My name is Ashwin, and I am born and brought up in Kerala, a small state in India. We speak Malayalam at home, a complicated yet amazing language with 52 alphabets… I did schooling in my hometown--Calicut, in a few modest schools, and moved to Cochin city for pursuing Bachelors Degree in Mechanical Engineering at the Cochin University. I did okay there and secured a couple of job offers through campus recruitment programmes: at IBM--a huge and successful company at its peak, with very good pay and then at Emerson Network Power, with R&D position and half the salary when compared to IBM. After so much contemplation, I joined Emerson, with a view to go for Masters’ degree after acquiring work experience in Mechanical Engineering--which started it all.

I worked as a Design engineer in the Mechanical Engineering department of an electric system manufacturing company. Due to the heavily understaffed manpower in its Mechanical division, I had to singlehandedly work towards developing mechanical enclosure for a power conditioning system: a big project and one of the first original product from the R&D division. Naturally, I was under a lot of pressure, and the work was quite hectic, starting from 8 AM in the morning till 9 at night (if I get lucky).. While I was extremely disappointed, exhausted and depressed of my work back then, in the retrospect, I am so happy that I lived through that experience because unknowingly, I learned the entire process of developing an industrial product from the conceptualization stage till production.
Though I could stay in touch with “Mechanical Engineering” in Emerson, I couldn’t crack the entrance tests for Masters programme (GATE exam). My attempts were futile due to my inability to prepare for the exams due to the work schedule. I however, managed to crack an entrance exam to BITS Pilani university and secured admission in its Goa campus for Masters Degree, and that too, in Design Engineering!

I was nervous during my admission to BITS, because the university is one of the best private universitites in the country and I had forgotten much of the basics that I learned during my bachelors. But again, in some sense it helped me since I had the opportunity to start learning things afresh! The quality and manner of teaching was totally different and way better than my undergraduate programme. Also I had this amazing friend Allen, who was a genius and a go-getter. With his help, I rediscovered the process of learning and eventually fell in love with it. I still remember the very first paper-based test that I took from BITS after a gap of over 2 years. I was quite freaked out for the first 15-20 minutes that I couldn't write anything even though I knew how to solve them! But from there, I picked myself up and did quite good for every other tests and graduated as the batch topper. Well, I missed the full 10 GPA only because of the low marks in that very first test!

It was from BITS that Allen made me realize that marks and grades are just numbers and what really matters is how much you understand stuff. Also I had a motivating advisor, Prof. Raghavendra Datta Naik who was eager to conduct interesting research in the field of non-linear dynamics, vibrations and control systems. Under his guidance, I got the first taste of research. And I liked it. I liked the satisfaction and the joy of daring to read research papers and understanding the concepts which I otherwise believed was too cryptic to apprehend. It might have taken a whole day for me to understand a three-line derivation, but the sheer joy of grasping the concept and playing with it inside my head was unparalleled! By the time I was about to graduate, I was fully immersed in conducting research and all my studies were focused on aiding this quest. With heavy influences from Allen and Prof. Naik, I was sure that I would pursue a career in research after my Masters.

Me and Allen applied to a bunch of top universities for Ph.D. positions from BITS. Taking advice from a veteran professor in my department, Prof. B.J.C. Babu, I dropped my plans to attempt to go outside the country for a Ph.D. It made sense to me back then, when he convinced me that conducting research in India is as good as conducting it from anywhere else since I am focussed towards theoretical work. (But honestly, now I realize that in fact I missed the chance of building an academic network and a better exposure which I would have had if gone abroad). So, instead of joining the offered Ph.D. position in a University in Taiwan, I proceeded to join IISc Bangalore. That selection was also based on a brief interaction with Prof. Rudrapratap who bowled me over with his charisma and his way of explaining why I should conduct research from India and that too in IISc instead of elsewhere. By the end of an all India trip hopping from IIT Bombay to IIT Kanpur to IISc Bangalore and finally IIT Madras, Allen decided to join IIT-Bombay for his Ph.D. while I decided to join IISc Bangalore.
To me, the joy which follows solving a problem, or understanding its nature has indeed been a river whose burst decided by the complexity of the task. And more when I get to see an original concept springing to life.
